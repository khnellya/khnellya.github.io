Cultural Events. Exploring Women's Voices in Cultural Narratives

Welcome!

We are thrilled to invite you to our website dedicated to exploring the profound narratives of women within cultural heritage. Our project, "Exploring Women's Voices in Cultural Narratives," integrates the ArCo knowledge graph with advanced technological tools to delve into this captivating subject. Driven by our passion for women’s rights and cultural representation, our team endeavors to uncover and celebrate the impactful stories of Esther Lurie and Jacqueline Monica Magi within the ArCo framework. These cultural events represent pivotal moments in history, highlighting resilience, activism, and artistic expression.

Our project encompasses a comprehensive approach, from crafting intricate SPARQL queries to analyzing data insights using state-of-the-art language models. By creating new knowledge triples and exploring thematic connections, we aim to enrich the understanding of women’s contributions in cultural narratives. Join us on this intellectual journey as we delve deep into the intersection of cultural heritage and women’s voices, utilizing modern technologies to illuminate their significance in history and society.

Let’s explore it together:

Theme 1: Esther Lurie We explore cultural events and narratives related to Esther Lurie, particularly focusing on her artistic contributions and experiences during the Holocaust.

Theme 2: Jacqueline Monica Magi - "Bambine negate" We investigate cultural events centered around Jacqueline Monica Magi's work "Bambine negate," which addresses issues of gender-based violence through personal narratives and advocacy.
